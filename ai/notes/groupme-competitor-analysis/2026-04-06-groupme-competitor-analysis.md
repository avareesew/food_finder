# Competitor Analysis: GroupMe "BYU Free Food"

**Date:** April 6, 2026
**Context:** Thomas raised in class that a GroupMe already exists for BYU free food alerts. This note documents our due diligence.

---

## What It Is

**BYU Free Food** is a GroupMe school group with ~2,793 members. Students post about free food events on campus in a shared chat. The group description reads: *"Share events with free food! Mute certain channels if you need. Be kind, and please participate in the events and not just take food & bounce."*

It is active — last activity was the same morning we investigated it.

---

## Friction Points (Observed Firsthand)

### 1. No anonymity — BYU email + profile photo required
GroupMe requires a BYU email to join the school group, and a profile photo must be uploaded. Students who just want to grab free food between classes may not want their identity attached. Scavenger requires no login and no profile — fully anonymous browsing.

### 2. Permission-gated access — join request still pending after 4 days
Joining requires admin approval. We submitted a join request and it was still **Pending** 4 days later. Students looking for food *today* can't wait 4 days to get access. Scavenger is open immediately — no request, no wait.

### 3. One flat chronological feed — no map, no filtering
GroupMe is a chat interface. Posts scroll by in a single feed with no way to filter by building, time, or food type. There is no map. Students have to scroll through messages to find something relevant to them. Scavenger surfaces events on an interactive campus map with location and time at a glance.

---

## Scavenger's Differentiation Summary

| Feature | GroupMe "BYU Free Food" | Scavenger |
|---|---|---|
| Account required | Yes (BYU email + photo) | No |
| Anonymity | No | Yes |
| Access speed | Pending approval (days) | Instant |
| Discovery UI | Flat chat feed | Interactive map |
| Filtering | None | By location, time |
| Posting | Manual by anyone | Auto-ingested from Slack/email |

---

## Takeaway

GroupMe "BYU Free Food" validates the demand — 2,793 members is strong signal. But the friction (required identity, gated access, no spatial discovery) leaves a clear gap that Scavenger is designed to fill. We are not replacing GroupMe; students who are already in that group will likely use both. But students who want low-commitment, anonymous, map-based discovery have no current option.
