## Interview 1 ‚Äî Kendall Castlelaw
**Past Finance Society President ¬∑ Marriott School of Business (Tanner Building)**  
**Date:** 2026-02-24

### Q: How do you currently promote events?

- **Digital screens** throughout the Tanner Building
- **Newsletter** distributed to all freshmen (Marriott School)
- **Instagram accounts** tied to the Tanner Building (Marriott policy-managed)
- **In-class announcements** ‚Äî go into prerequisite classes to announce events directly
- **‚ö†Ô∏è No physical flyers in Tanner** ‚Äî you get in trouble for distributing flyers at the Tanner unless it is a large, Marriott-wide event (Marriott School policy)

### Q: How often did you order food for events?

- Finance Society ordered food for **every event**
- Companies would frequently **sponsor the food** (~80% of events were company-sponsored)

### Q: What happens to leftovers?

- Usually taken home ‚Äî *"take home a pizza for your roommates"*
- Very rarely did they have food that students didn't grab ‚Äî demand was typically high at their events

### Q: Would you use a platform to post leftover food?

- **Main barrier:** Wanting to give food to students who came to the event. The food is paid for by Finance Society dues-paying members, so the preference is to serve members first.
- Not opposed to others coming, but the dynamic is: *"this is for students that came to our event"*
- Open to it if framing is right ‚Äî **"any exposure is good exposure"**; fine with non-members showing up for food if they're willing to sit through the event

### Q: What concerns would you have about liability / safety?

- No concerns raised. Covered by the exposure comment above ‚Äî happy for anyone to attend.

## Key Insights & Implications

### üö® Critical Finding: Tanner Building is digital-first, not flyer-first
The Marriott School of Business prohibits physical flyer distribution in the Tanner Building except for large, school-wide events. Promotion there runs through **digital screens, Instagram, and newsletters** ‚Äî not bulletin board flyers. This directly challenges the assumption that 70%+ of BYU club events are promoted via physical flyers.

**Impact on roadmap assumption:**
- "BYU clubs print physical flyers" may be false for the Marriott School cluster entirely
- Need to validate whether this policy is isolated to Tanner or applies more broadly
- The AI flyer-scanning pipeline may have lower supply from one of campus's largest club ecosystems

**Suggested action:** In the flyer survey (Task 0.2.1), specifically note how many flyers come from Marriott vs. other buildings. If Tanner is empty of flyers, pivot toward digital/manual entry for that segment.

---

### üí° Supply-Side Motivation is More Nuanced Than Expected
The assumption was that organizers want to reduce waste and will gladly post leftovers. The reality is subtler: **organizers feel a primary obligation to their own members** (who funded the food via dues). Posting publicly feels like diverting a member benefit to outsiders.

**The opening:** Kendall noted "any exposure is good exposure" ‚Äî meaning posting works if it's framed as recruitment/event promotion, not just waste reduction. Framing it as *"get people in the door"* rather than *"give away your leftovers"* may be more effective for this persona.

**Impact on PRD:** The Club Recruiter persona should emphasize attendance and club visibility, not just waste reduction. The "bring food, attract future members" angle resonates more than the sustainability angle for this user.

---

### ‚úÖ Liability Concern Validated as Low
No concerns raised. Aligns with the 2023 FDIA positioning in the PRD. The legal shield framing is confirmed as a non-issue for at least this persona.

---

### üìä Food Frequency Data Point
80% of Finance Society events had company-sponsored food. This is a strong signal that food events are common ‚Äî but it also means the food "belonged" to a sponsor, which may add another layer to liability framing (sponsor's food, not the club's).
