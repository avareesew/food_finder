## Interview 1 — Michael Nichols

**President · BYU Sales Society**
**Date:** 2026-04-06

### Background / Context Delivered

Scavenger framed as a live, map-based web app that turns "free food" alerts into a professional recruitment tool by integrating with official club communications, verifying posts to prevent troll entries, and providing students a frictionless/anonymous way to discover the club's mission, location, and menu.

### Club Overview

- Michael has been involved for 3 years: VP of Communications → President
- Club recently became an official association (no longer a sub-club of Marketing)
- Primary goal: educate students on B2B professional sales, shifting the "sleazy" stereotype toward high-level careers
- 20+ corporate partners; companies pay thousands to present — quality of student engagement is paramount

### Current Marketing Stack

1. **Email** — two emails/week during peak semester to 500–600 person list
2. **Instagram** — main page + official BYU "School Story" (accessible via .edu verification)
3. **Class Shoutouts** — leadership visits major business classes (MKTG 201, ECON 110)
4. **⚠️ No Slack** — not part of their current stack

### Food at Events

- Food at **every single event** (unlike many clubs)
- Food is matched to the "vibe" of the visiting company (e.g., healthy salads when Just Ingredients visits, not pizza)
- Prefer Chick-fil-A for catering; the student who picks it up keeps the reward points (VP of Events perk)
- Avoid "messy" food like Panda Express (rice/noodles on the floor)
- Expect **75–100 people** at bi-weekly trainings; track attendance consistently

### Falsification Tests

**Effort vs. Reward (5–10 min to post manually):**
→ Would use it. Even if it only brought in 1–2 "high-quality" students whose lives were changed by the career training, the 10-minute effort is worth it.

**Professionalism Tension:**
→ **Major concern.** *"I'd rather have half the people show up and all of them be engaged than double the people and half are on their phones."* Views food as a reward for staying, not a grab-and-go service.

**Student Stigma:**
→ No. Admitted being part of "food groups" as a freshman. Doesn't think students feel stigma, but also doesn't think an anonymous map would significantly change grab-and-go behavior.

### Solution Feedback

**Automation (zero-effort background sync):**
→ *"That would be awesome."* Loved that his VP of Training wouldn't need to do extra work.
→ Noted this would be a big win especially for **sub-associations** (smaller niche sales groups) with smaller budgets and higher food frequency.
**Rating: 10 / 10**

**Malicious Entry Prevention (pulling from official channels):**
→ Hadn't considered troll posts before, but liked the security.
→ Primary concern: feeling bad for students who show up to a fake event and are disappointed — not brand damage.

**Killer Feature (info students NEED before walking in):**

1. **Behavioral Expectations:** "Stay the whole time," "Be courteous," "Bring something to take notes on."
2. **Company Info:** Students need to know which of the 20 companies is presenting so they can come prepared to network.

### Key Synthesis

- The high level of **corporate involvement** (companies pay thousands) means student **engagement quality** is infinitely more important than attendance quantity.
- **New feature required:** A "Professionalism Badge" or "Expectations" toggle. For clubs like Sales Society, hide the specific food type until a student clicks "I will attend and stay for the duration" — to filter out low-engagement food hunters.

**Key Quote:**

> "I'd rather have half the people and all of them be engaged... if this helps us find those one or two students who really want to learn, it's worth it."

## Interview 2 — Molly Wakefield

**VP · Finance Society**
**Date:** 2026-04-06

### Club Overview

- Finance Society helps underclassmen understand finance career paths and get into the finance major
- Food is **not at every event** — strictly reserved for Opening and Closing Socials due to budget constraints
- Guestimate food quantity from historical data (e.g., 30 pizzas for fall, ~25 for winter when Marriott is slightly less busy)

### Current Marketing Stack

1. **Instagram**
2. **Weekly emails**
3. **TV monitors** in the Marriott School
4. **⚠️ Explicitly does not use Slack**

### Falsification Tests

**Effort vs. Reward (5–10 min to post manually):**
→ Would spend 5–10 minutes manually. Would go up to **20 minutes** if it guaranteed doubling attendance.

**Professionalism Tension:**
→ Concern exists. The Deans of the Marriott School are currently pushing a **"professional manners" initiative**. Worry that a "food-only" app could conflict with the goal of having students show up for content, not just pizza.

**Student Stigma:**
→ Doesn't believe there's major stigma. Used her twin brother as an example — he'd show up for food regardless of "the look." Acknowledged a small 5–10% of students might feel awkward.

### Solution Feedback

**Automation (pull from official emails / calendar):**
→ Major win. Especially helpful for sub-associations under Finance Society — smaller groups with food more frequently.
→ Also valuable for drawing in **upperclassmen** who are harder to reach but valuable for mentoring underclassmen.
**Rating: 9 / 10**

**Malicious Entry Prevention:**
→ Not a major concern. Believes students would use "a few brain cells" to spot a fake post compared to their official Instagram.

**Killer Feature (info students NEED before walking in):**

1. **QR code integration** to sign up for the club email list or interview for positions
2. **Expectation setting:** Must stay for the full event; show up on time (they often serve food at the **end** of events to ensure engagement)

### Key Synthesis

- The tension between **recruitment numbers vs. the Marriott "Professional Manners" initiative** is real. Scavenger must be framed as a **professional discovery tool**, not just a "free food" app, to satisfy faculty/dean requirements.
- **New feature:** A "Requirements" field on event pins (e.g., "Must stay for duration," "Business Casual") helps club leaders maintain professional standards while still using food as a draw.

**Key Quote:**

> "The automation is a win, especially for the sub-associations. It helps us get those upperclassmen back in the room to mentor the younger students."

## Interview 3 — Abigail Armstrong

**President · Women of the School of Accountancy**
**Date:** 2026-04-06

### Club Overview

- Mission: Help women network and prepare for the professional accounting field
- National accounting grads are 60% female; BYU is only 30% — the club actively works to close that gap and retain women in the program
- Fully funded by the School of Accountancy (free for students to join); budgets must be approved by the school — **the real "customer" is the faculty, not the student**
- 6–7 events per year, planned at the start of each semester; afternoon events = lunches, evening events = desserts
- Orders based on prior-year attendance averages

### Current Marketing Stack

1. **Physical flyers** — go up about a week before the event
2. **Emails** — sent to current members irregularly (~monthly)
3. **In-class pitches** — most effective channel; goes directly into Acc 200 and Acc 310
4. **⚠️ No Slack** — not mentioned

### Food at Events

- Food at **every single event** to drive attendance
- Food choice directly impacts turnout: Jimmy John's → 50 people (expected 75); Chick-fil-A → 80 people

### Falsification Tests

**Hypothesis A — Manual Entry (would you use it consistently even if it required login + new post every week?):**
→ 100% yes. Even a 25% attendance bump is worth the effort because attendance fluctuates so much and getting people in the room is the top priority.

**Hypothesis B — Branding (does it bother you if students show up only for food?):**
→ "Getting them in the door" is the only metric that matters. *"I am 100% willing to bribe students with food"* — she just needs the chance to give her 10-minute professional spiel. Female students are generally courteous enough to stay at least 10 minutes after getting food.

**Hypothesis C — Stigma (would an anonymous map lower awkwardness?):**
→ Yes. If club leaders cannot see the specific names of individuals who found the event through the app, it removes the stigma/awkwardness entirely.

### Solution Feedback

**Automation (listen to Slack/Email and auto-pin to map):**
→ High value — 10/10.
→ **⚠️ New risk flagged:** Potential mandate for clubs to move to **Microsoft Teams + Outlook** for official communications. Other presidents say the requirement is just "official channels" (so Slack may still qualify), but this is **unconfirmed** and needs to be verified.
**Rating: 10 / 10**

**Malicious Entry Prevention:**
→ Security is good, but **"BYU red tape"** is a bigger concern. Every flyer must be approved by two BYU faculty members. If Scavenger can show a post is "BYU Official," it helps navigate that administrative approval pressure.

**Killer Feature (info students NEED before walking in):**

1. **Scarcity info:** "Food for the first 100 people" — creates urgency and sets expectations
2. **"Join the Club" button:** A direct sign-up link on the map pin would help prove attendance/membership value to the School of Accountancy for budget approvals

### Key Synthesis

- **The real customer for "Associations" is the faculty budget approver**, not the student. The club must justify its existence to the school; a "Join Association" button on event pins directly helps with that.
- **BYU red tape / official approval** is a real constraint — framing Scavenger as verifying "BYU Official" posts (pulled from official channels) is a meaningful institutional sell, not just a trust signal for students.
- **New feature:** Add a **"Club/Association Link"** field on event pins — direct sign-up or interest form URL — to convert food visitors into registered members and help presidents prove ROI to their funding bodies.
- **Teams/Outlook integration may be needed** — unconfirmed mandate; needs follow-up to determine if Slack will remain an accepted "official channel."

**Key Quote:**

> "I am 100% willing to bribe students with food... even if this only increases our attendance by 25%, it's worth it for us to get our mission in front of them."

## Cross-Interview Insights & Implications

### ✅ Automation Is the Killer Feature — Validated (3/3, avg 9.7/10)

All three interviewees rated automatic ingestion from official channels at 9–10/10. This is the clearest product validation to date. Zero-effort posting via email/Slack sync is not a nice-to-have — it's the primary reason club leaders would adopt Scavenger.

**Impact on pivot story:** The automation angle (not the map, not the feed) is the supply-side unlock. Emphasize this in the final presentation.

---

### ⚠️ Professionalism Tension Is Real — Confirmed (2/3, nuanced)

Sales Society and Finance Society independently raised concerns about food hunters. Women of Accountancy takes the opposite view — "getting them in the door" is the only metric; food is explicitly used as a bribe and that's fine. The tension is real for corporate-facing clubs but less so for mission-driven recruitment clubs.

**Impact on product:**

- Add a **"Requirements" / "Expectations" field** to event pins (both interviewees asked for this independently)
- Consider a **"Professionalism Toggle"** that hides food specifics until a student acknowledges behavioral expectations (Sales Society specific)
- Frame Scavenger as a **professional discovery tool**, not a food-waste app

---

### 💡 Sub-Associations Are a Key Beneficiary Segment — New Finding (2/2)

Both interviewees specifically called out sub-associations / smaller clubs under their umbrella as the biggest beneficiaries: smaller budgets, higher food frequency, less marketing bandwidth.

**Impact:** Consider targeting sub-association leaders as early adopters. They have the most to gain and the lowest bar for adoption.

---

### 📣 Engagement Quality > Attendance Quantity (Sales Society)

Companies pay thousands to present. The club acts as a recruiter. One high-quality attendee > ten disengaged ones.

**Impact on framing:** The "bring food, attract future members" angle must be paired with quality filtering. The product should help clubs attract *the right* students, not just more students.

---

### 📱 Slack Is NOT Universal (Finance Society confirmed)

Finance Society explicitly does not use Slack. Their stack is Instagram + email + TV monitors. The Slack ingestion pipeline (recently built) is not applicable to all clubs.

**Impact:** Email ingestion must be a first-class path. Slack is additive, not universal.

---

### 🎯 "Club Link" on Event Pins — Requested (2/3)

Both Finance Society (QR code / email signup) and Women of Accountancy (direct "Join Association" button) asked for a way to convert food visitors into registered members directly from the pin.

**Impact:** Add an optional **"Club Link" URL field** to event pins. Simple, high-value for clubs that need to demonstrate membership ROI to funding bodies.

---

### 🏛️ Institutional Approval Is a Real Constraint (Women of Accountancy — New Finding)

Every flyer requires approval from two BYU faculty members. For school-funded Associations, the real "customer" is the faculty budget approver, not the student. Framing Scavenger posts as "BYU Official" (pulled from verified channels) isn't just a student trust signal — it helps clubs navigate administrative red tape.

**Impact:** Lean into the "verified from official channels" framing in institutional pitches, not just student-facing copy.

---

### ⚠️ Microsoft Teams / Outlook Integration — Unconfirmed Risk (Women of Accountancy)

Abigail mentioned a potential mandate for BYU clubs to move to Teams + Outlook. Other presidents say the requirement is just "use official channels," so Slack likely still qualifies — but this is unverified.

**Impact:** Research both Outlook/Teams and Slack integrations. The email ingestion path already covers Outlook. Confirm with BYU club administration whether Slack is still an accepted official channel.
