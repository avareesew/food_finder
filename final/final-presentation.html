What is Scavenger?

A mobile-first web platform that turns campus food events into a live, searchable map — with no login required for students.

1 Club leaders submit food event info to Scavenger
2 AI extracts the event details — time, location, food type
3 Events are pinned to a live campus map in real time — visible to any student, anonymously
🎯

For Club Leaders

Round 1 Interviews

The Assumptions Started Breaking

What we found

🚫 Tanner Building bans physical flyers — Marriott School policy. Email, Instagram, and digital screens only.
📉 Leftover supply is low — clubs order conservatively; food rarely goes to waste.
🎯 Motivation is recruitment, not waste reduction — "any exposure is good exposure."

What this meant

The flyer-scanning pipeline targets a supply that barely exists — in the Tanner Building, one of the largest hubs for club events on campus.

We needed to rethink the whole supply side.

Product Response

Addressing the Presidents' Concerns

🔗

Club Landing Page Link

Presidents need food visitors to actually join the club — not just grab pizza and leave. A direct sign-up link on every event pin converts foot traffic into members and helps clubs prove ROI to their budget approvers.

"A direct link to sign up for the club — right on the pin."
— Abigail Armstrong, Women of Accountancy
📋

Behavioral Expectations Field

Corporate-facing clubs worry about food hunters who grab food and leave. Every event pin includes an optional "Expectations" field — clubs can require students to stay for the full event, dress business casual, or come prepared to network.

"Must stay for duration" · "Business casual" · "First 100 students only"
— Examples from Sales Society + Women of Accountancy
Both features were requested independently by 2 of 3 club presidents — they weren't prompted.

Document-Driven Dev

PRD → mvp.md → roadmap → implementation. Every sprint planned in writing before code was touched. AI onboards from context.md — no repeated explanations.

🌿
Git Workflow

Conventional commits: feat/fix/docs/chore. The ai/ folder is committed — roadmaps, changelogs, and interview notes are visible in git history. No committed secrets.

🔁
Test → Log → Fix Loop

ESLint enforces structured logging — no raw console.log in src/. Every ingestion event is a traceable JSON log. AI diagnoses from logs, fixes, and documents in changelog.

📖
CLAUDE.md + Context

Behavioral rules live in CLAUDE.md — enforced across every session. context.md gives the AI a current-state snapshot so each session picks up without re-explaining the project.

Appendix · Business Model

How Scavenger Could Make Money

🎯
Club Subscription

Clubs pay a small monthly fee to post. More attendees = more recruits = more corporate partners. A fraction of their catering budget.

~$10–30/mo per club · 50 clubs = $500–1,500 MRR
⭐
Freemium Analytics

Basic posting is free. Premium unlocks attendance estimates, repeat visitor tracking, and "Join Club" conversion data. Sells itself to presidents proving ROI to budget approvers.

Free tier drives adoption · paid tier monetizes power users
📣
Ad Revenue

At scale, the student-facing map is high-intent campus traffic. Local restaurants and recruiting companies pay for sponsored pins or placements.

Low-friction at scale · doesn't affect the free student experience
Why clubs will pay: Scavenger's supply-side users (club leaders) already spend money on food to attract members. A platform that automates posting and measurably increases attendance is not a cost — it's a force multiplier on their existing catering budget.
