What is Scavenger?

A mobile-first web platform that turns campus food events into a live, searchable map — with no login required for students.

1 Club leaders post to their existing email or Slack channels — nothing new to learn
2 Scavenger automatically ingests those posts via AI text extraction
3 Events are pinned to a live campus map in real time — visible to any student, anonymously

The Original Hypothesis

Where We Started

41%
of college students face food insecurity
Tons
of catered food wasted on campuses each semester

We believed the problem was a supply–demand mismatch: clubs have leftover food, students have food insecurity, and the two never connect.

Our original solution

1 Club leaders upload a flyer photo after an event
2 AI extracts event details from the image (OpenAI vision)
3 Pin appears on a campus map for students to find leftover food
Framing: reduce food waste + address food insecurity
Round 1 Interviews · Feb 2026

The Assumptions Started Breaking

What we found

🚫 Tanner Building bans physical flyers — Marriott School policy. Email, Instagram, and digital screens only.
📉 Leftover supply is low — clubs order conservatively; food rarely goes to waste.
🎯 Motivation is recruitment, not waste reduction — "any exposure is good exposure."

What this meant

"Fine with non-members showing up for food if they're willing to sit through the event."
— Kendall Castellaw, Finance Society (Past President)

The flyer-scanning pipeline targets a supply that barely exists — at the biggest club building on campus.

We needed to rethink the whole supply side.

Competitive Analysis

GroupMe Validates the Market — and Leaves a Gap

2,793
members
in BYU Free Food GroupMe — demand is real

Their friction points (observed firsthand)

1 No anonymity — BYU email + profile photo required just to see posts
2 Access takes days — our join request was still Pending after 4 days
3 Flat chronological chat — no map, no filtering by location or time
Feature GroupMe Scavenger
Account required Yes No
Anonymous browsing No Yes
Access speed Days Instant
Discovery UI Flat chat Map + feed
Posting Manual, anyone Auto-ingested
We are not replacing GroupMe. Students already in it will likely use both — but we serve everyone who won't jump through those hoops.
Student Survey · April 2026 · n=9

Students Are Ready for This

4.0
out of 5 avg likelihood to check a live food map before/between classes
78%
said anonymity makes it feel less awkward ("no account, no profile")
67%
find out about free food events after they've already happened

Top info students want before walking in

  • Behavioral expectations — "Do I have to stay?" (top answer, 7/9)
  • Specific food type — Chick-fil-A vs. pizza matters (6/9)
  • Dress code — business casual vs. casual (5/9)
  • Scarcity / "First 100 people" — creates urgency (2/9)

Students aren't just looking for free food — they're managing social risk. The anonymity and expectations fields directly address that.

How We Build It

From Manual Upload to Automated Ingestion

Before

Anyone could manually upload a flyer

Anyone visits site
→
Uploads flyer image
→
AI extracts event
⚠️ Security gap: No verification. Anyone could post anything — troll posts, fake events, bad info.
Now

Automatic ingestion from official channels

Club emails their list
→
AI reads inbox
→
Map pin created
✅ Zero effort for clubs. Posts come from verified official channels — troll posts are structurally impossible.

Manual upload still available — but only for verified club accounts approved by admins.

What's Next

Next Steps

Alpha — Target segment

1 Sub-association leaders first — smaller budgets, higher food frequency, most to gain from automation
2 Seed 10–15 real events with design partner clubs to prove the feed has value
3 Expand beyond Marriott — validate that the email ingestion model works for non-business school clubs

Product — Open items

  • "Mark as Gone" toggle — prevent ghost chases
  • Requirements / Expectations field on pins
  • Club Link field — convert visitors to members
  • Microsoft Teams / Outlook integration (unconfirmed BYU mandate)
  • Real-time onSnapshot feed updates
